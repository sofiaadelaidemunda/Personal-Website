My Film Movies

Romance / Drama Favorites

Romance / Drama

Four Sisters and a Wedding (2013)

Director: Cathy Garcia-Molina
Stars: Angel Locsin, Toni Gonzaga, Bea Alonzo, Shaina Magdayao, Enchong Dee

Four sisters reunite to stop their younger brother’s wedding, leading to humorous but heartfelt confrontations about family, love, and unresolved resentments.

She’s Dating the Gangster (2014)

Director: Cathy Garcia-Molina
Stars: Kathryn Bernardo, Daniel Padilla

A high school girl pretends to date a campus bad boy to make his ex jealous, but their fake romance turns into something real and bittersweet.

Love You to the Stars and Back (2017)

Director: Antoinette Jadaone
Stars: Julia Barretto, Joshua Garcia

A quirky young woman embarks on a road trip to alien‑hunt, but instead finds love and healing with a boy battling cancer.

Seven Sundays (2017)

Director: Cathy Garcia-Molina
Stars: Aga Muhlach, Dingdong Dantes, Cristine Reyes, Enrique Gil

Four estranged siblings are forced to reunite when their father is diagnosed with cancer, leading to heartfelt confrontations, healing, and rediscovery of family bonds.

Youth / Coming-of-Age

Drawing Closer (2024)

Director: Takahiro Miki
Stars: Ren Nagase, Natsuki Deguchi

A teenage artist with one year left to live meets a girl with only six months left, and together they find meaning in love and art.

100 Tula Para kay Stella (2025)

Director: Jason Paul Laxamana
Stars: Bela Padilla, JC Santos, Kyle Echarri

Fifteen years after their youthful romance, Stella and Fidel reconnect, confronting past wounds and the possibility of rekindled love.

Historical / Adaptation

Little Women (2019)

Director: Greta Gerwig
Stars: Saoirse Ronan, Emma Watson, Florence Pugh, Eliza Scanlen, Timothée Chalamet

The March sisters navigate love, ambition, and independence in 19th‑century Massachusetts, reimagining Louisa May Alcott’s classic novel.

Pride & Prejudice (2005)

Director: Joe Wright
Stars: Keira Knightley, Matthew Macfadyen, Rosamund Pike, Donald Sutherland

Elizabeth Bennet navigates love and societal pressures in Regency‑era England, sparring with the enigmatic Mr. Darcy in this adaptation of Jane Austen’s classic novel.
